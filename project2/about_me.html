About Me

Life began in the Upper Left - The Pacific Northwest. It is some of the most beautiful country in the United States. Just look...

Every winter of my youth was spent skiing at the mountain in that picture. It was the best! My parents both volunteered their time to the local Ski Patrol, so that we could ski for dirt cheap. As such, I got to ski every weekend like I was some kind of rich kid. I wasn't. My parents were public servants - my dad worked in education, my was a nurse, my stepdad was a cop, and my dad's wife sometimes worked clerical jobs. But, we were rich with opportunity. Being on Ski Patrol meant that we got to be some of the first people on the mountain, and the last people down the slopes. Even when the weather was shit, I skied... because that was the job. I didn't really mind. I loved skiing. Besides skiing in bad conditions taught me resiliency.

Family legend is that my Dad threw me into a backpack and took me on my first runs down the mountain at just 6 weeks old. There is tangible documentation of my first go down the slopes by myself. It occured when I was just 3 years old. Needless to say, I became a pretty good skier at a relatively young age. I could ski a double black diamond before I could do long division. By the age of thirteen, I had skied every run at that mountain before. That's when I started to ski anything that would fit two skis. I would duck under the ropes and explore beyond the designated runs of the ski area. Wooded areas, frozen waterfalls, small cliffs,. I skied it all. Nothing made me happier than skiing.

Whereas winter meant skiing, fall was meant for playing soccer. As summer came to a close, I would lace up the cleats and hit the soccer pitch. I played for 20 years. In the beginning it was just a fall sport, but in my late teens I was playing year-round. I started when I was just 5 years old. I was never as strong of a soccer player as I was a skier.

I played soccer in High School. At least, I played until my Senior year, when the coach explained to me that I wouldn't get a starting position on the Varsity team because it was going to a Freshman. "Team growth," he said. So, I decide to focus my efforts on club ball. I got to travel and play with people from different countries. It was pretty cool. When I quit the High School team, my friends in the art room (Art was my other lifelong passion( convinced me to try theater. So I auditioned for a play.

I got hooked... bad.

The moment I stepped on stage for my first live show I was in love. There was something addictive about the exchange between actor and audience. The wave of energy was palpable as swelled from the house to the stage. I loved that exchange.

I did all the plays for my last year of High School. I even had a role in the winter musical despite my deep seeded fear of singing in public. My transition from jock to theater nerd was worthy of a John Hughes film. But I guess I was still playing club soccer during much of the year. My teacher/director forced me to audition for a scholarship at some theater competition. Despite COMPLETELY fucking up that audition, I was somehow gifted a scholarship to a small liberal arts college. Over the next year, theater would consume my life.

I continued to play soccer, but only in competitive rec-leagues. Most of my attention was placed on the pursuit of "my acting career". Unfortunately, nothing had prepared me to actually pursue a career in acting. One year I skipped Mother's Day for a soccer match and blew out my knee. I have never been able to play again. It also meant that I could no longer ski. In one afternoon, I lost two of the activities that shaped my youth. Fortunately, I still had theater... so I made it my life.

It didn't happen right away. I struggled with the lose of my athleticism for several years. It wasn' until a casting director in Phoennix, the instructor took the group to an improv comedy show. Improvisation was like soccer and theater came together to make one art form just for me. It was SO MUCH fun. In my first class, I got a big laugh from the other students in the audience. It was a RUSH. The energy exchange in a scripted performance was addictive, but improvisation was like the crack cocaine of theater. I fell hard for improvisation. From that day forward, I devoted all my attention, time, and efforts into mastering the artform.

Everything good in my life has come from improvisation. I am now a performer and instructor at one of the best improvisational theaters in the world. I get to teach students from around the world. I got hired by the Museum of Science and Industry in Chicago to infuse improvisation into their culture. That job lead to travelling the country using improvisational techniques to help educators and scientists better communicate with the general public. I met my wife at the museum. We now have two amazing children together. Improvisation lead me to museums, and there I found my future career.

I returned to school so that I could get the skills to create computer interactive in museum exhibits. I still improvise and teach improvisation, but I know that my career is not in theater. I look forward to getting back to education and museums. I look forward to using my new skills acquired at Illinois Institute of Technology to build exhibits that engage kids (and adults) in really cool science.
